# Eastell & Co — Design Principles

Derived from Atollon client analysis: Kollosche, BresicWhitney, Marshall White.

## Brand Tokens

- Black: `#010101`
- Dark Grey: `#58585b`
- Grey: `#a7a9ac`
- Cream: `#f4e5d8`
- Light: `#faf6f2`
- Display font: Engravers Gothic (all caps, wide tracking)
- Body font: Hoefler Text (classic serif, warm)
- Tone: "Premium without being cold. Confident without being arrogant."

## Core Principles

1. **Headlines are 2-5 words max.** Not sentences. Not paragraphs. "knows Sydney." "Luxury begins here." Radical confidence.
2. **Photography does the heavy lifting.** The images carry the site. Copy is minimal. If the photo isn't stunning, the section fails.
3. **No cheesy stats bars.** None of the premium agencies use "$180M settled" counters. Trust is communicated through presence, not numbers.
4. **Property-forward.** Real listings shown immediately, not buried below filler sections.
5. **Massive whitespace.** Let the work breathe. Density kills luxury.
6. **Warm but confident tone.** Never use "premier", "exceed expectations", "unmatched", or any other template-speak.
7. **Editorial lifestyle content.** Journal/magazine sections, community stories — not just listings.
8. **Simple navigation.** Buy / Sell / About / Team / Contact. Nothing more.
9. **No clutter.** Every element must earn its place. If it doesn't serve the user, cut it.
10. **Architecture-first imagery.** Interiors, structure, light — not "luxury mansion with pool" stock photos.

## Reference Sites

- **Kollosche** (kollosche.com.au) — "Luxury begins here." Integrated hero search. Media logos. Suburb profiles with lifestyle copy. Emotional CTA.
- **BresicWhitney** (bresicwhitney.com.au) — "knows Sydney." Two words. Recently listed carousel. 4-tile navigation grid. Journal with cultural content. DatoCMS. Zero clutter.
- **Marshall White** (marshallwhite.com.au) — "Leaders in prestige property." Property-first homepage. Tabbed listings. "60 years of excellence" editorial block. Open Magazine. DatoCMS.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

- Generic Unsplash stock photos (same house on every real estate template)
- Stats bars with made-up numbers
- "Sunshine Coast's premier real estate agency" — template filler copy
- "We live here, raise our families here" — cliche
- Reusing the same image in multiple sections
- Scroll-triggered animation on every single element
- Copy that could apply to any agency in any city
